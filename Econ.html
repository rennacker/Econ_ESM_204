  2. Benefits are unclear for owners of old cars because old does not mean that it is worth less than $2000. If a car is worth more than 2000 dollars than it would not be beneficial for owners to sell back to the state. But if the old car is worth less than 2000 than it would stand to reason that these cars would come out of the woodwork and off the road. This policy may also supply more money to the used car market, because owners of older cars may be priced out of the new car market and they may take their rebate back into the used car market driving prices up for older cars further limiting the amount of cars available to the state at the price of 2000.
